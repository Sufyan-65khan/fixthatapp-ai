Brawl Stars Account Locked: 7 Fixes

Last updated: March 22, 2026

You open Brawl Stars and instead of loading your village, the game greets you with a suspension message or silently logs you into a brand-new guest account — leaving all your brawlers, trophies, and gems seemingly gone. For many players this is terrifying, especially those who've spent months building up their roster. Brawl Stars account locks fall into two broad categories: Supercell ID session issues (easily fixed) and genuine bans or security locks (require appeals or verification). The good news is that nearly all account access problems have a clear resolution path. This guide covers every scenario — from a simple re-login to emergency guest account recovery and ban appeals.

Quick Diagnosis: What Type of Lock Are You Facing?

Game opens a new guest account instead of yours → Supercell ID session expired. Start with Fix 1.

"Your account has been suspended" message appears → Temporary or permanent ban. Go to Fix 4.

Supercell ID email or password not accepted → Password issue or account lockout. See Fix 2.

Lost access to email linked to Supercell ID → Manual verification needed. See Fix 3.

Account vanished after reinstalling the app → Likely a guest account without Supercell ID. See Fix 5 urgently.

Received a ban notification email from Supercell → Follow the appeal process in Fix 4.

7 Fixes for Brawl Stars Account Locked

Fix 1: Re-Sign Into Your Supercell ID

Why this works: Brawl Stars uses Supercell ID as its cloud authentication system. After a reinstall or major update, the app can lose its active session and default to a blank guest account. Your real account is still on Supercell's servers — it just isn't connected to this session. Re-linking your Supercell ID retrieves all your progress instantly.

  1. Open Brawl Stars. If a new account appears, do not play on it.
  2. Tap the gear icon (Settings) in the top-left of the home screen.
  3. Scroll down and tap Connected Accounts.
  4. Tap Connect next to Supercell ID.
  5. Enter your Supercell ID email and password and sign in.
  6. When prompted whether to use this Supercell ID, select Use this Supercell ID — your original account will load.

Fix 2: Reset Your Supercell ID Password

Why this works: If your password was changed — either by you forgetting it or following a security compromise — you'll need to reset it via Supercell's website. Supercell uses email verification to confirm identity, so you need access to the linked email address for this step.

  1. Visit supercell.com/en-us/supercell-id in a browser.
  2. Click Sign In, then select Forgot Password.
  3. Enter your registered email address.
  4. Check your inbox for a reset link from Supercell (also check spam/junk).
  5. Set a new strong password, then use it to sign into Brawl Stars via Connected Accounts.

Fix 3: Recover a Lost Email Account or Update Your Supercell Email

Why this works: If you no longer have access to the email tied to your Supercell ID, Supercell support can manually verify your identity and migrate the account to a new email. This process requires you to provide account details that only the real owner would know.

  1. First attempt to recover your email via your email provider's account recovery page.
  2. If recovery is impossible, go to supercell.helpshift.com/a/brawl-stars/ and contact support.
  3. Select Account as the category and explain you've lost email access.
  4. Provide: your player tag (shown in your in-game profile), device type, approximate account creation date, and any Google Play or App Store purchase receipts.
  5. Supercell will verify ownership and update the email on your Supercell ID.

Fix 4: Appeal a Brawl Stars Ban

Why this works: Supercell's automated anti-cheat system can produce false positives. All bans are subject to appeal, and Supercell does overturn incorrect bans when evidence supports it. Submitting a well-documented appeal gives you the best chance of reinstatement.

  1. Go to supercell.helpshift.com/a/brawl-stars/.
  2. Select Contact Us and choose the account ban category.
  3. Provide your player tag, the email linked to your Supercell ID, and a clear explanation of why the ban is a mistake.
  4. Include screenshots or any evidence demonstrating you were not violating the Terms of Service.
  5. Submit the ticket and wait — Supercell typically responds within 2 to 5 business days.

Do not submit multiple tickets for the same issue; this resets your position in the queue.

Fix 5: Emergency Recovery for a Lost Guest Account

Why this works: Guest accounts are stored locally on your device with no cloud backup. If you reinstalled Brawl Stars without first linking a Supercell ID, the account appears to vanish. Supercell may be able to recover it using device identifiers if you act quickly.

  1. Contact Supercell support at supercell.helpshift.com/a/brawl-stars/ immediately — the sooner the better.
  2. Select "I lost my account" and describe what happened.
  3. Provide: your old device's model and OS version, the player name or approximate tag, purchase receipts from the App Store or Google Play, and the approximate date you last played.
  4. Do not play on the new guest account — any activity on it complicates recovery.

Fix 6: Secure a Compromised Account

Why this works: If someone else gained access to your Supercell ID and caused the account to be banned, or if suspicious logins triggered Supercell's automatic lock, you need to secure the account before Supercell will restore access.

  1. Change your Supercell ID password immediately at supercell.com/en-us/supercell-id.
  2. Change the password on the email address linked to your Supercell ID.
  3. Enable two-factor authentication on your email account.
  4. Contact Supercell support to report the compromise and request a security review of your account.

Fix 7: Reinstall Brawl Stars and Reconnect

Why this works: App data corruption can sometimes cause login loops where Supercell ID fails to connect even with correct credentials. A clean reinstall resolves local app issues without affecting your cloud-saved account — provided it is linked to Supercell ID.

  1. Confirm your account is linked to Supercell ID before proceeding.
  2. Uninstall Brawl Stars from your device.
  3. Reinstall from the App Store or Google Play.
  4. Open the game, tap Settings gear, then Connected Accounts, and sign in with your Supercell ID credentials.

What NOT to Do

Common mistakes that make this worse
  • Don't reinstall Brawl Stars without first linking a Supercell ID. Reinstalling the app on a device without a linked Supercell ID permanently loses access to any guest account. Your trophy count, Brawlers, and purchases are unrecoverable without the Supercell ID link. Link the account before you delete or change devices — there's no exception to this.
  • Don't share your Supercell ID credentials with anyone offering to "recover" your account. Third-party account recovery for Brawl Stars does not exist. Anyone asking for your login credentials is attempting to steal your account. All real account recovery happens through Supercell's official support at supercell.helpshift.com.
  • Don't submit multiple support tickets for the same lost account. Supercell's support system flags duplicate tickets, which can delay your case or cause it to be closed as a duplicate. Submit one detailed ticket with your player tag, device info, and purchase receipts, then wait for a response before following up.
  • Don't log into your Supercell ID on a new device before the old device issue is resolved. Logging into a Supercell ID on a new device immediately overwrites whatever account was on that new device. If you're troubleshooting a locked or lost account, do this step deliberately and only after confirming your Supercell ID still has the correct account linked.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I recover a Brawl Stars guest account lost after reinstalling?

A: Guest accounts without a Supercell ID are extremely difficult to recover. Contact Supercell support at supercell.helpshift.com immediately with your device model, approximate player tag, and any purchase receipts. Supercell may restore it using device identifiers, but success is not guaranteed — this is why linking a Supercell ID is critical.

Q: Why was my Brawl Stars account banned without warning?

A: Supercell's automated systems flag accounts for cheating, use of unauthorized third-party apps, account boosting, or purchasing accounts from third parties. If you believe it is a mistake, appeal via supercell.helpshift.com. Supercell reviews all appeals and overturns incorrect bans when the evidence supports it.

Q: How long does a Brawl Stars temporary account lock last?

A: Temporary locks typically last 24 to 72 hours, triggered by suspicious login activity or too many failed password attempts. After the lock lifts, immediately set a stronger password and enable two-factor authentication on the email linked to your Supercell ID.

Q: I lost access to the email on my Supercell ID — what now?

A: First try recovering the email through your provider's recovery process. If that fails, contact Supercell support with your player tag, device info, and purchase receipts. Supercell can manually verify your identity and migrate your Supercell ID to a new email address.

Q: Is it safe to move Brawl Stars to a new phone?

A: Yes, as long as your account is linked to Supercell ID. Install Brawl Stars on the new phone, tap the settings gear, select Connected Accounts, and sign in with your Supercell ID. All brawlers, trophies, gems, and Brawl Pass progress transfer instantly.

Still Stuck?

If none of these fixes resolved your account issue, contact Supercell directly through their official support portal at supercell.helpshift.com/a/brawl-stars/. Include your player tag, the email associated with your Supercell ID, your device information, and any relevant purchase history to help Supercell verify and restore your account faster.
