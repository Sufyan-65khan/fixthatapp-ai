# Troubleshooting Guide: Fitbit Not Syncing

## Problem Description
Your Fitbit device may stop syncing with the Fitbit app, meaning your steps, heart rate, sleep data, and other health metrics aren't being transferred to your phone. The app may show outdated data or fail to connect to the device.

## Possible Causes
1. **Bluetooth disabled**: Fitbit syncs via Bluetooth.
2. **Fitbit app outdated**: Older versions may have sync bugs.
3. **Device too far from phone**: Bluetooth has a limited range.
4. **Battery low**: Low battery on your Fitbit can prevent syncing.
5. **Background app refresh disabled**: The Fitbit app needs to run in the background.

## Step-by-Step Fixes

### Fix 1: Check Bluetooth
- Make sure Bluetooth is turned on: Settings > Bluetooth > Toggle On.
- Keep your Fitbit device close to your phone (within 15 feet).
- Disable Bluetooth, wait 10 seconds, and re-enable it.

### Fix 2: Force Sync
- Open the Fitbit app.
- Pull down on the dashboard to force a sync.
- Wait up to 30 seconds for the sync to complete.

### Fix 3: Restart Your Fitbit
- For Fitbit watches: Press and hold the button(s) for 10 seconds until you see the logo.
- For trackers: Plug into the charger and hold the button for 10 seconds.
- Try syncing again after restart.

### Fix 4: Restart Your Phone
- Power off your phone completely.
- Turn it back on and open the Fitbit app.
- Try syncing again.

### Fix 5: Remove and Re-add Your Fitbit
- Open the Fitbit app > Account > Your Device > Remove This Device.
- Restart both your phone and Fitbit.
- Re-add the device through the Fitbit app.

### Fix 6: Update the Fitbit App
- Go to the App Store or Google Play Store.
- Update the Fitbit app to the latest version.
- Open the app and try syncing.

## When to Contact Support
If syncing still doesn't work, contact Fitbit support at help.fitbit.com or through the Fitbit app's Help section.

## FAQ
Q: Will I lose my data if my Fitbit doesn't sync?
A: Fitbit devices store data locally for several days. Once you successfully sync, all stored data will transfer. Don't factory reset your device before syncing.

Q: How often should my Fitbit sync?
A: Fitbit should sync automatically throughout the day when your phone is nearby with Bluetooth on. You can also manually sync by opening the app.

Q: Can I sync Fitbit with multiple phones?
A: Your Fitbit can only be connected to one account and one primary phone at a time. Switching phones requires setting up the device again.

Q: Why does my Fitbit sync with my phone but not show updated data?
A: The app may need to refresh. Pull down on the dashboard, or close and reopen the app. Also check that you're viewing today's data and not a previous day.
