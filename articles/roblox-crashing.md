# Troubleshooting Guide: Roblox Crashing

## Problem Description
Roblox may crash unexpectedly during gameplay, on startup, or when loading a specific game. You might see error messages, the app may freeze, or it may close without warning.

## Possible Causes
1. **Device doesn't meet requirements**: Roblox requires minimum hardware and software specifications.
2. **Outdated graphics drivers**: Old GPU drivers are a common cause of Roblox crashes.
3. **Corrupted game files**: Damaged installation files can cause instability.
4. **Too many background processes**: Insufficient memory can lead to crashes.
5. **Incompatible software**: Antivirus or overlay software may conflict with Roblox.

## Step-by-Step Fixes

### Fix 1: Check System Requirements
- Roblox requires Windows 7 or later, macOS 10.13 or later, or a recent mobile device.
- At least 1 GB of RAM is needed (4 GB recommended).
- A dedicated graphics card improves stability.

### Fix 2: Update Graphics Drivers
- On Windows: Open Device Manager > Display Adapters > Right-click your GPU > Update Driver.
- Visit your GPU manufacturer's website (NVIDIA, AMD, or Intel) for the latest drivers.
- Restart your computer after updating.

### Fix 3: Clear Roblox Cache
- Press Win+R, type `%temp%` and press Enter.
- Delete all Roblox-related temporary files.
- On Windows, you can also delete the Roblox folder in %localappdata%\Temp.

### Fix 4: Reinstall Roblox
- Uninstall Roblox from your device.
- On Windows: Settings > Apps > Find Roblox > Uninstall.
- Restart your computer, then reinstall from roblox.com.

### Fix 5: Disable Antivirus Temporarily
- Some antivirus programs flag Roblox as suspicious and interfere with it.
- Temporarily disable your antivirus and test if Roblox runs without crashing.
- If it works, add Roblox to your antivirus exceptions list.

### Fix 6: Lower Graphics Settings
- In Roblox, press Esc > Settings.
- Set Graphics Mode to "Manual" and lower the Graphics Quality.
- This reduces the load on your system and can prevent crashes.

## When to Contact Support
If Roblox continues crashing, visit en.help.roblox.com to submit a support request. Include your device specs, error messages, and the specific game that causes crashes.

## FAQ
Q: Why does Roblox crash on my phone?
A: Your phone may not have enough RAM or processing power. Close other apps, lower Roblox graphics settings, and make sure your phone isn't overheating.

Q: Does Roblox work on Chromebooks?
A: Roblox is available on some Chromebooks through the Google Play Store. If it crashes, make sure your Chromebook's OS is up to date.

Q: Why does only one specific Roblox game crash?
A: The game itself may have bugs or may be too resource-intensive for your device. Try other games to confirm it's game-specific, then report it to the game developer.

Q: Can I get banned for Roblox crashing?
A: No, you won't get banned for crashing. However, if you crash during a game, you may lose progress in that session.
