# Troubleshooting Guide: Dropbox Files Not Syncing

## Problem Description
Dropbox may stop syncing files, showing a paused icon, "Syncing" that never completes, or files not appearing across your devices. This can cause you to work with outdated files or lose recent changes.

## Possible Causes
1. **Storage full**: Your Dropbox account may be out of space.
2. **Sync paused**: Syncing may have been manually paused.
3. **Internet connection**: Poor connectivity prevents syncing.
4. **Conflicting files**: Files with the same name edited on different devices.
5. **Firewall blocking**: Network security may block Dropbox.
6. **Selective sync settings**: Some folders may be excluded from syncing.

## Step-by-Step Fixes

### Fix 1: Check Sync Status
- Click the Dropbox icon in your system tray/menu bar.
- If sync is paused, click "Resume Syncing".
- Look for any error messages or warnings.

### Fix 2: Check Storage Space
- Visit dropbox.com/account to see your storage usage.
- Free accounts get 2 GB. If full, delete files or upgrade your plan.
- Empty the Deleted Files section to free up space.

### Fix 3: Check Internet Connection
- Make sure you're connected to the internet.
- Try uploading a small file manually to dropbox.com.
- Switch networks if your current one is unstable.

### Fix 4: Check Selective Sync
- Click Dropbox icon > Profile > Preferences > Sync.
- Click "Selective Sync" and make sure all needed folders are checked.
- Unchecked folders will not sync to your computer.

### Fix 5: Restart Dropbox
- Click the Dropbox icon > Profile > Quit Dropbox.
- Wait 10 seconds and reopen Dropbox.
- Check if syncing resumes.

### Fix 6: Reinstall Dropbox
- Uninstall Dropbox from your computer.
- Download the latest version from dropbox.com/install.
- Sign in and let it re-sync your files.

## When to Contact Support
If syncing still doesn't work, visit help.dropbox.com to contact Dropbox support. Plus and Professional subscribers get priority support.

## FAQ
Q: Will reinstalling Dropbox delete my files?
A: No, all files are stored in the cloud. Reinstalling only removes the desktop app. Your files will re-sync when you reinstall.

Q: Why does Dropbox show "conflicted copy"?
A: This happens when the same file is edited on multiple devices before syncing. Dropbox keeps both versions. Compare them and delete the one you don't need.

Q: How much free storage does Dropbox give?
A: Dropbox Basic (free) gives 2 GB. You can earn extra space by referring friends or completing setup steps.

Q: Can I sync specific folders only?
A: Yes, use Selective Sync in Dropbox Preferences to choose which folders sync to your computer.
